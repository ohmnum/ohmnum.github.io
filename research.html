Research

A guided tour of what I work on, with the key papers behind each theme explained in plain terms. The full list lives on the publications page.

01

Prognostics & Health Management

Detecting, diagnosing, and predicting faults in rotating machines, motors, and thermal power plants — so failures are caught early and maintenance happens before something breaks.

  1. SCOC: Stator Current Operation Compensation for PMSM fault diagnosis

    Measurement ’23First author

    A preprocessing step that compensates a motor’s stator-current signals for changing speed and load, so a deep model can diagnose permanent-magnet synchronous motor (PMSM) faults reliably even under variable operating conditions.

  2. MPARN: Multi-scale Path Attention Residual Network for rotating machines

    JCDE ’23First author

    A residual network with multi-scale paths and attention that captures fault signatures across different time scales, improving fault diagnosis of rotating machinery.

  3. Opt-TCAE: boiler-tube leakage detection in a thermal power plant

    ESWA ’23First author

    An unsupervised temporal convolutional auto-encoder that flags boiler-tube leaks from multi-sensor signals; the latent size is tuned by entropy minimization and the alarm threshold is set automatically via kernel density estimation.

  4. Self-supervised feature learning for motor fault diagnosis under various torque conditions

    Knowledge-Based Systems ’24

    Self-supervised pre-training learns motor-fault representations without labels, so diagnosis stays accurate across a wide range of torque conditions with little labeled data.

  5. FL-SSDAN: Fleet-level semi-supervised domain adaptation for overhead hoist transports

    JCDE ’25Co-first author

    Transfers fault knowledge across many overhead-hoist-transport units in a semiconductor fab using mostly unlabeled data, so a model trained on a few machines generalizes to the whole fleet.

  6. Center-margin loss, uncertainty-aware diagnosis for unseen faults

    JCDE ’26acceptedFirst author

    Adds uncertainty estimation and a center-margin loss so the model can flag novel, never-before-seen fault types instead of forcing them into known classes.

  7. Frequency-band graph-based sensor fusion for machinery fault diagnosis

    Eng. Appl. of AI ’26

    Fuses multiple sensors on a frequency-band graph with a sensitivity-aware “energy assist” network, sharpening fault diagnosis of complex machinery systems.

02

Domain Knowledge Informed Neural Networks

End-to-end deep learning often ignores the physics and signal structure engineers already know — which hurts generalization, interpretability, and physical consistency. DINN folds that knowledge back in: physics-based loss terms, signal-aware inputs (e.g. 2D time–frequency maps), and training across simulated conditions.

  1. A review of PHM methods based on domain-knowledge-informed neural networks

    in progressFirst author

    Defines DINN, organizes the ways physics, signal analysis, and expert rules can be fused into PHM models, analyzes their limits, and maps out open directions.

03

AI-driven Design

Using AI to generate designs, predict performance faster than simulation, and search high-dimensional design spaces via inverse modeling and reinforcement learning.

  1. Rapid, precise die design to compensate springback in metal bipolar-plate stamping

    under reviewFirst authorw/ Fraunhofer IPT

    A neural inverse model that predicts the stamping/die parameters needed to hit a target metal bipolar-plate geometry for PEM fuel cells — compensating springback in seconds instead of through iterative simulation. A collaboration with Fraunhofer IPT (Aachen).

  2. Reinforcement-learning-based optimal parameter estimation of an ultra-precision machine

    ICEAS ’22First author

    Frames process-parameter tuning of an ultra-precision machine as a reinforcement-learning problem to reach optimal settings efficiently.

04

LLM Applications in Engineering

Current work at Samsung Research: domain-specialized LLMs, knowledge graphs, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) that bring engineering data and physical reasoning into one workflow.

  1. LLM-based engineering design framework

    in development

    An LLM workflow that generates candidate designs and folds in feedback, aiming to fit user requirements while respecting physical constraints — bridging fragmented simulation and optimization tools.

  2. Knowledge-graph + RAG + LLM for PHM

    research

    Represents fault-related knowledge as a graph and keeps a PHM assistant accurate and up to date by combining retrieval-augmented generation with LLMs — addressing the black-box and stale-knowledge problems of plain models.
